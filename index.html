Oncogenic Viruses

Oncogenic viruses can cause cancer by disrupting host cell genetics. This encyclopedia provides detailed information on their biology, causes, prevention, and research.

What are Oncogenic Viruses? Oncogenic viruses, also known as tumor viruses, are viruses that can cause or contribute to the development of cancer in humans and animals. They do this by integrating their genetic material into the host cell's DNA, which can disrupt normal cell regulation and lead to uncontrolled cell growth.

Examples: Some well-known oncogenic viruses include Human Papillomavirus (HPV), Hepatitis B Virus (HBV), Hepatitis C Virus (HCV), Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV), Human T-cell Leukemia Virus type 1 (HTLV-1), and Kaposi's Sarcoma-associated Herpesvirus (KSHV).

How do they cause cancer? These viruses may produce proteins that interfere with tumor suppressor genes or activate oncogenes, leading to the transformation of normal cells into cancerous ones.
